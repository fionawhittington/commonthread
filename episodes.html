Sanctuary Campus

Listen to us talk with Marwa Sayed, a student activist who launched a petition late last year to make BU a 'sanctuary campus'. We define actions cities and campuses can take, discuss the potential limitations and delve into the way activism is changing by the day as events unfold. If you want to zoom in on the grassroots in the Trump Era, spin this Thread.

Dispatch from Calais

After sixteen years of existence, the refugee camp known as "the Jungle" of Calais, Northern France was dismantled in October leaving hundreds of men, women, and children in a precarious position as their dream to reach the UK was put on halt. We interview BU student Smaranda Tolosano who visited the “Jungle” of Calais a few days before its evacuation and followed the early stages of the government-orchestrated shutdown.

Mental Health on College Campuses - Ep 3

We interview director of Behavioral Medicine at SHS, Dr. Carrie Landa. We talk about some of the functions of Behavioral Medicine, including how appointments are prioritized, the purpose of BU's services, and the programs SHS is creating to educate the student body and faculty about mental health awareness and communication.

Mental Health on College Campuses - Ep 2

We talked to a senior psychology major from BU, Sravya Vajram. She discusses the group she founded, SpeakUP, an independent and student-run support group on campus. As well, we discuss the importance of being vulnerable and confiding in our peers.

Mental Health on College Campuses - Ep 1

In this episode we interview recent Psychology grad from BU, Emme Parks. She discusses the group she founded, All for One, along with her experience with Behavioral Medicine at BU.

Martin Luther King Jr.

Martin Luther King Jr. is tragically misrepresented as a simple idealist who offered moving oratory. In truth, he was much more than this. He was a revolutionary leader who evolved to face the challenges posed to his movement and offered radical proposals to solve the plight of the poor. Listen to us discuss the final years of his life based on extensive research and conversations with Reverend Julian Cook and Mr. Ryan Hendrickson.
